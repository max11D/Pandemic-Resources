Mental Health Crisis Resources

The resources on this page are intended to provide people in a mental health crisis with a trained person to talk to.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

"The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a national network of local crisis centers that provides free and confidential emotional support to people in suicidal crisis or emotional distress 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. [They're] committed to improving crisis services and advancing suicide prevention by empowering individuals, advancing professional best practices, and building awareness."

Phone: (800) 273-8255

Website: suicidepreventionlifeline.org

Crisis Text Line

"Every texter is connected with a Crisis Counselor, a real-life human being trained to bring texters from a hot moment to a cool calm through active listening and collaborative problem solving. All of Crisis Text Line's Crisis Counselors are volunteers, donating their time to helping people in crisis."

Text "Home" to 741-741

Website: crisistextline.org
